Iowa Falls is a city located in Hardin County, Iowa, United States. Often referred to as "The Scenic City," it serves as a regional transportation center situated along U.S. Routes 20 and 65, with rail service provided by Canadian National and Union Pacific Railroads. The city is also home to Ellsworth Community College, a notable educational institution in the region.

The area now known as Iowa Falls was originally called Rocksylvania, but the name was changed due to difficulties with pronunciation and spelling. The city was formally laid out and platted in 1856, taking its name from the falls on the Iowa River. The Illinois Central Railroad was built through the city in 1866, contributing to its growth as a transportation hub. In 1899, the Metropolitan Opera House was constructed, serving as a venue for plays, operas, and vaudeville performances throughout the first half of the twentieth century. This building is now listed on the National Register of Historic Places. Another cherished local landmark is the Iowa Falls Popcorn Stand in Estes Park, recognized as the oldest continuously operating popcorn stand in Iowa. It first opened on May 29, 1948, under the name "Louie's Korn Krib."

Geographically, Iowa Falls sits at the point where the Iowa River flows through a limestone gorge approximately 70 feet deep, flanked by bluffs rising an additional 50 feet. The falls, historically known as the "rapids of the Iowa," are now submerged by a dam constructed in 1926. This dam stands 26 feet high and stretches 200 feet long, powering a hydroelectric station that currently generates 1.5 megawatts of electricity. According to the United States Census Bureau, the city covers a total area of 5.44 square miles, of which 5.36 square miles is land and 0.08 square miles is water. The climate is classified as hot-summer humid continental under the Koppen Climate Classification system, designated "Dfa." Record temperatures range from a high of 111 degrees Fahrenheit in July to a low of negative 34 degrees Fahrenheit in January. The annual daily mean temperature is approximately 46.7 degrees Fahrenheit.

According to the 2020 United States Census, Iowa Falls had a population of 5,106 people living in 2,146 households and 1,235 families. The population density was 948.1 inhabitants per square mile. There were 2,422 housing units at an average density of 449.7 per square mile. The racial composition was 89.1 percent White, 1.1 percent Black or African American, 0.4 percent Native American, 0.9 percent Asian, 3.4 percent from other races, and 5.1 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino residents of any race made up 6.6 percent of the population. Among the households, 26.1 percent had children under 18, and 42.3 percent were married couples living together. Non-family households constituted 42.5 percent of the total, with 37.3 percent consisting of individuals living alone and 19.8 percent being individuals aged 65 or older living alone. The median age was 40.7 years. Residents under 20 made up 24.1 percent, those between 25 and 44 accounted for 21.8 percent, and those aged 65 or older comprised 23.7 percent. The gender split was 48.6 percent male and 51.4 percent female.

The 2010 census recorded a population of 5,238, slightly higher than the 2020 figure, indicating a modest decline over the decade. At that time the racial makeup was 94.0 percent White, 2.7 percent African American, 0.4 percent Native American, and 0.7 percent Asian, with Hispanic or Latino residents at 3.9 percent. The average household size was 2.17 and the average family size was 2.81. The median age was 40.9 years.

Data from the 2000 census showed a population of 5,193 with 2,215 households and 1,331 families. The population density at that time was 1,043.1 inhabitants per square mile. The city was 97.34 percent White, with Hispanic or Latino residents comprising just 1.04 percent. The median household income was 32,141 dollars and the median family income was 42,279 dollars. Males earned a median income of 31,216 dollars compared to 21,004 dollars for females. Per capita income stood at 18,330 dollars. Approximately 7.1 percent of families and 10.0 percent of the total population lived below the poverty line, including 12.5 percent of those under 18 and 8.3 percent of those aged 65 and older.

Iowa Falls holds several economic and institutional features of note. Iowa Select Farms, the largest hog producer in Iowa, maintains its headquarters in the city. Public education is provided through the Iowa Falls Community School District. Local media includes KIFG, known as Iowa River Radio, broadcasting on 95.3 FM and 1510 AM. Notable individuals associated with Iowa Falls include NBA player Nick Collison, Tuskegee airman Joseph Gomer, former NFL players Kurt Ploeger and Randy Schultz, television personality Bill Riley Sr., Hollywood director John Whitesell, and Hollywood agent Patrick Whitesell, who served as co-CEO of WME Entertainment. The city's combination of natural beauty along the Iowa River gorge, its historical landmarks, and its role as a regional transportation and educational center continue to define its identity within central Iowa.
