Eldora is a city and the county seat of Hardin County, Iowa, United States. The city was platted in 1853 and officially incorporated on July 1, 1895. The name Eldora was given by a local mother in honor of her deceased infant daughter. The name itself is derived from a Spanish term meaning "the gilded." Eldora carries the motto "Come and Explore" and serves as the administrative center for Hardin County. Its ZIP code is 50627.

According to the United States Census Bureau, Eldora has a total area of 4.33 square miles, which is equivalent to 11.21 square kilometers. The entire area is classified as land, with no water area recorded. The city is situated on the eastern edge of the Altmont Moraine, a glacial moraine that marks the eastern border of the Des Moines Lobe of the Wisconsin Glaciation. Eldora lies just west of a gorge carved by the Iowa River along the border of the moraine, where the river has cut through the sandstone bedrock underlying the glacial formation.

The climate of Eldora is classified as humid continental under the Koppen Climate Classification system, specifically subtype Dfa, which denotes a hot summer continental climate. The region experiences large seasonal temperature differences, with warm to hot and often humid summers and cold, sometimes severely cold, winters. Precipitation is distributed relatively evenly throughout the year. Based on climate normals from 1991 to 2020, the mean daily maximum temperature ranges from 25.8 degrees Fahrenheit in January to 83.5 degrees Fahrenheit in July. The annual mean daily maximum is 57.4 degrees Fahrenheit. The daily mean temperature averages 46.9 degrees Fahrenheit annually, and the mean daily minimum temperature ranges from 8.4 degrees Fahrenheit in January to 61.9 degrees Fahrenheit in July, with an annual average of 36.5 degrees Fahrenheit.

The 2020 United States Census recorded a population of 2,663 people in Eldora. The city contained 1,096 households and 632 families. The population density was 622.0 inhabitants per square mile, or 240.2 per square kilometer. There were 1,276 housing units at an average density of 298.1 per square mile. The racial composition was 89.6 percent White, 2.3 percent Black or African American, 0.2 percent Native American, 0.8 percent Asian, 0.1 percent Pacific Islander, 1.7 percent from other races, and 5.3 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino persons of any race made up 3.6 percent of the population. The median age was 43.3 years. The age distribution showed 27.3 percent under 20, 3.9 percent aged 20 to 24, 19.8 percent aged 25 to 44, 25.3 percent aged 45 to 64, and 23.7 percent aged 65 and older. The gender ratio was 50.7 percent male and 49.3 percent female. Among the 1,096 households, 24.9 percent had children under 18, 43.5 percent were married couples, 7.6 percent were cohabitating couples, 28.6 percent had a female householder without a spouse, and 20.3 percent had a male householder without a spouse. Non-family households constituted 42.3 percent, with 36.7 percent consisting of individuals living alone and 19.4 percent being persons aged 65 or older living alone.

Eldora has experienced a gradual population decline over recent decades. The 2010 census recorded 2,732 residents, down from 3,035 in 2000. In 2010, the population density was 630.9 per square mile, and the racial composition was 94.8 percent White, 2.0 percent African American, 0.3 percent Native American, 0.6 percent Asian, 0.7 percent from other races, and 1.6 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino residents comprised 3.7 percent. The median age was 41.6 years, with 51.7 percent male and 48.3 percent female. The average household size was 2.27 and the average family size was 2.87.

From the 2000 census, the median household income was 33,170 dollars, and the median family income was 39,421 dollars. Males had a median income of 31,545 dollars compared to 17,891 dollars for females. The per capita income was 15,459 dollars. Approximately 5.9 percent of families and 6.9 percent of the total population lived below the poverty line, including 6.6 percent of those under 18 and 2.9 percent of those 65 and older.

Eldora is served by the Eldora-New Providence Community School District, which was formed on July 1, 1980, through the merger of the Eldora and New Providence school districts. The district maintains a grade-sharing arrangement with the Hubbard-Radcliffe Community School District and operates under the name South Hardin Schools. South Hardin High School is located in Eldora, while South Hardin Middle School is in Hubbard. The Eldora Herald-Ledger is a weekly newspaper covering Eldora and Hardin County, printed on Tuesdays by the Hampton-based Mid-America Publishing Corporation since November 2014.

The city is located adjacent to the Iowa River and Pine Lake State Park. Eldora is also the site of the State Training School for Boys, a facility for male juvenile delinquents. Notable individuals associated with Eldora include George C. Armstrong, an Illinois state senator; George Gardner Fagg, a federal judge on the United States Court of Appeals for the Eighth Circuit; Jonathan Hole, a vaudeville and film actor; and Mona Van Duyn, winner of the 1991 Pulitzer Prize for Poetry and United States Poet Laureate from 1992 to 1993. The city gained additional recognition as a filming location for the 1996 movie Twister, with the climactic tornado scene shot at the old Follett house in Eldora.
