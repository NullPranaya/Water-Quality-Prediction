Coralville is a city in Johnson County, Iowa, United States, and a suburb of Iowa City. It is part of the Iowa City Metropolitan Statistical Area. The population was 22,318 at the 2020 census. The city carries the informal nickname "CoralVegas" and uses the ZIP code 52241.

Coralville incorporated as a city on June 1, 1857. The city's name is derived from the fossilized coral found in the limestone along the Iowa River. In 1864, Louis Agassiz, a Harvard University zoologist, delivered a lecture at the nearby University of Iowa titled "The Coral Reefs of Iowa City," in which he presented local samples of fossilized Devonian period coral. The lecture generated public interest in the local fossils, and in 1866, when more corals were discovered at the site of a new mill, citizens named the settlement Coralville. The first mill at Coralville was built in 1844, and a number of watermills were powered by the Coralville mill dam along the Iowa River in the years that followed. All of the mills had closed by 1900, except for a low-head hydroelectric plant that remained in operation until the mid-twentieth century.

Coralville is also the location of the Edgewater Park Site, a 3,800-year-old archaeological site along the Iowa River. It is the oldest site in Iowa with evidence of domesticated plant use. Additionally, the area served as an encampment for approximately 1,300 Mormon immigrants in 1856 during their westward migration. Having traveled by rail to Iowa City, the westernmost rail terminus at the time, they built handcarts from native woods at Coralville so that adults could haul loads of 600 to 700 pounds and cover about 15 miles per day on foot on their journey to Salt Lake City. A historical marker commemorating the Mormon Handcart Brigade was erected in 1936, and today the Mormon Handcart Park and Nature Preserve marks the site.

After World War II, Coralville began to grow as university students settled there. The city had only 433 people in 1940, but by 1970 its population had jumped to 6,130. The construction of Interstate 80 in the 1960s brought motels, fast-food restaurants, and gas stations to the area, accelerating commercial development. In 1958, the United States Army Corps of Engineers completed Coralville Dam along the Iowa River, four miles north of the city, creating Coralville Lake. The dam has helped prevent serious flooding, except during the Great Flood of 1993 and the Great Iowa Flood of 2008. During the 1993 flood, all three of Coralville's main transportation links with Iowa City were submerged. The 2008 flood proved even more costly, with the Iowa River surpassing the 1993 record crest at over 31.5 feet.

According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 12.05 square miles, of which 12.01 square miles is land and 0.04 square miles is water. The Iowa River runs along the east edge of Coralville and forms part of the boundary with Iowa City. Interstate 80 runs east to west through the city, with most newer housing subdivisions located north of the highway. U.S. Route 6 runs along the south edge, while Interstate 380, U.S. Route 218, and Iowa Highway 27 run along the western edge.

The 2020 census recorded 22,318 people living in 9,584 households. The population density was 1,776.3 inhabitants per square mile. The racial makeup was 70.2 percent White, 13.9 percent Black or African American, 9.4 percent Asian, 0.2 percent American Indian and Alaska Native, 0.1 percent Native Hawaiian or other Pacific Islander, and 5.5 percent identifying with two or more races. Hispanic or Latino persons of any race comprised 4.2 percent. Among households, 39.7 percent were married couples, 30.9 percent had a female householder with no spouse present, and 20.9 percent had a male householder with no spouse present. The average household size was 2.24 persons. Approximately 15.3 percent of residents were born outside the United States. The median age was 32.6 years, with 20.3 percent under 18 and 13.0 percent over 65. The gender split was 51.2 percent male and 48.8 percent female. The median household income from 2018 to 2022 was 67,691 dollars, the per capita income was 47,434 dollars, and 10.7 percent of residents lived in poverty.

The city experienced significant population growth across recent census cycles, rising from 15,123 in 2000 to 18,907 in 2010 and 22,318 in 2020. In 2000, the median household income was 38,080 dollars and the median family income was 57,869 dollars, with 6.1 percent of families below the poverty line.

Coralville's retail economy expanded dramatically after the Coral Ridge Mall opened on July 29, 1998, with more than 100 stores, then the largest shopping center in Iowa. Taxable sales in the city grew from 155.3 million dollars in 1996 to 549.7 million dollars in 2006. The Xtream Arena, a 5,100-seat venue, opened in 2020 in the Iowa River Landing neighborhood and is home to the Iowa Heartlanders professional hockey team and the Coralville Chaos indoor football team.

Education in Coralville is served by the Iowa City Community School District, which annexed the independent Coralville school district by the mid-1960s. Elementary schools include Central, Kirkwood, and Wickham. Local bus service is provided by Coralville Transit, with commuter service to Iowa City and Cedar Rapids via the 380 Express. Notable individuals associated with Coralville include Samuel J. Kirkwood, former Governor of Iowa and United States Secretary of the Interior, and Nate Kaeding, a former NFL placekicker.
