Marengo is a city in and the county seat of Iowa County, Iowa, United States. It has served as the county seat since August 1845, predating its official incorporation in July 1859. The city was laid out in 1845 and platted in 1847. Its name commemorates the Battle of Marengo, in which Napoleon defeated the Austrian army. The population was 2,435 in the 2020 census, representing a decline from 2,535 in 2000. The city uses the ZIP code 52301 and carries the motto "Our town make it yours."

According to the United States Census Bureau, Marengo has a total area of 2.14 square miles, of which 2.08 square miles is land and 0.06 square miles is water. The Iowa River passes north of the town. The city is situated in Iowa County in the eastern part of the state.

The 2020 census recorded 2,435 people residing in 1,057 households and 615 families. The population density was 1,151.2 inhabitants per square mile. There were 1,147 housing units at an average density of 542.3 per square mile. The racial makeup of the city was 93.3 percent White, 0.2 percent Black or African American, 0.1 percent Native American, 0.5 percent Asian, 1.1 percent from other races, and 4.7 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino persons of any race constituted 4.1 percent of the population. The median age was 41.7 years. Among residents, 24.8 percent were under the age of 20, 5.8 percent were aged 20 to 24, 22.8 percent were 25 to 44, 25.5 percent were 45 to 64, and 21.1 percent were 65 or older. The gender makeup was 48.5 percent male and 51.5 percent female. Of the 1,057 households, 26.7 percent had children under 18, 41.4 percent were married couples living together, 7.8 percent were cohabitating couples, 30.8 percent had a female householder with no spouse or partner, and 20.0 percent had a male householder with no spouse or partner. Non-family households made up 41.8 percent, while 35.1 percent consisted of individuals living alone and 16.8 percent had someone aged 65 or older living alone.

The 2010 census recorded 2,528 people in 1,059 households and 648 families, with a population density of 1,215.4 inhabitants per square mile. There were 1,154 housing units at an average density of 554.8 per square mile. The racial composition was 97.3 percent White, 0.6 percent African American, 0.6 percent Native American, 0.4 percent Asian, 0.4 percent from other races, and 0.7 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino persons comprised 2.8 percent. Among households, 30.5 percent had children under 18, and 45.2 percent were married couples. Non-family households accounted for 38.8 percent. The average household size was 2.31 and the average family size was 2.94. The median age was 41 years. The gender breakdown was 47.9 percent male and 52.1 percent female.

The 2000 census counted 2,535 people in 1,057 households and 658 families. Population density was 1,210.5 inhabitants per square mile, with 1,135 housing units at a density of 542.0 per square mile. The racial makeup was 98.11 percent White, 0.28 percent African American, 0.04 percent Native American, 0.67 percent Asian, 0.28 percent from other races, and 0.63 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino residents made up 1.14 percent. Among households, 30.4 percent had children under 18, and 48.9 percent were married couples. The average household size was 2.32 and the average family size was 2.98. The median age was 38 years. The gender ratio showed 88.9 males for every 100 females, and 85.5 males for every 100 females aged 18 and over. The median household income was 36,509 dollars, and the median family income was 47,153 dollars. Males had a median income of 32,986 dollars compared to 21,401 dollars for females. The per capita income was 17,425 dollars. Approximately 6.1 percent of families and 7.3 percent of the population lived below the poverty line, including 6.5 percent of those under 18 and 11.9 percent of those aged 65 and older.

The population trend shows a gradual decline across the three most recent census periods, dropping from 2,535 in 2000 to 2,528 in 2010 and further to 2,435 in 2020. At the same time, the community has become slightly more racially diverse, with the White population decreasing from 98.11 percent in 2000 to 93.3 percent in 2020, and Hispanic or Latino representation rising from 1.14 percent to 4.1 percent over the same period.

The Iowa County Courthouse, built in 1893, is listed on the National Register of Historic Places. In 1859, abolitionist John Brown led a group of escaped slaves from Missouri to Michigan, with the group stopping at the Draper B. Reynolds Farm 1.5 miles south of Marengo on February 20 of that year. In December 2022, an explosion at the C6-Zero alternative fuel production plant resulted in a fire and multiple injuries, prompting an evacuation advisory until the toxic smoke cleared.

The Marengo Public Library is a Carnegie library constructed in 1905. Marengo is believed to be the smallest town in the United States to have received a donation from Andrew Carnegie for library construction. The Pioneer Heritage Museum features log cabins, a farmhouse, farm implements, household artifacts, clothing, and military history items from local residents. The city also features Rolle Bolle courts, a yard game known as krulbollen in Belgium, brought to the area by Belgian immigrants in the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries.

Local public schools include Iowa Valley Elementary School and Iowa Valley Junior Senior High School, both part of the Iowa Valley Community School District. Public safety services are provided by the Marengo Volunteer Fire Department, which has 25 members, and the Marengo Police Department, staffed by full-time, part-time, and reserve officers. Notable individuals from Marengo include Bradley Kasal, a Marine Corps Sergeant Major and Navy Cross recipient, Paul Hinrichs, a Major League Baseball relief pitcher, Jarrod Uthoff, a professional basketball player who played for the Dallas Mavericks, and Mildred Mott Wedel, a scholar of Great Plains archaeology and ethnohistory.
