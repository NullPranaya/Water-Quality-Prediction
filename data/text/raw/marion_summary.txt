Marion is a city in Linn County, Iowa, United States, located adjacent to Cedar Rapids and forming part of the Cedar Rapids Metropolitan Statistical Area. The city is situated at coordinates 42 degrees 2 minutes 25 seconds north latitude and 91 degrees 36 minutes 20 seconds west longitude, at an elevation of 840 feet. Marion is one of the fastest-growing cities in Iowa, ranking 14th in population statewide. Its ZIP code is 52302 and its area code is 319. The city website is cityofmarion.org.

Marion was named after Francis Marion, a hero of the American Revolutionary War. The site was selected in 1839 to serve as the first county seat of the newly organized Linn County, with the original survey completed in November of that year. The city was incorporated in 1837, predating its formal platting. After years of debate over relocating the county seat to the larger neighboring city of Cedar Rapids, a vote was held in 1919. The result was 9,960 in favor of moving the seat and 4,823 opposed, and Cedar Rapids became the new county seat. Marion also had historical connections to the railroad industry. Until 1971, the Milwaukee Road operated streamliner passenger trains making their Cedar Rapids regional stop at Marion station. The town was once home to St. Berchman's Seminary, established in 1905 by the Sisters of Mercy as a boarding school for boys. The academy closed in 1942 and consisted of five buildings spread over 23 acres. Actor Don Ameche lived at the facility as a boy before going on to star in the film Cocoon.

According to the United States Census Bureau, Marion has a total area of 16.06 square miles, of which 16.05 square miles is land and 0.01 square miles is water. The climate features a wide temperature range typical of central Iowa. Based on 1991 to 2020 normals, the annual daily mean temperature is 49.9 degrees Fahrenheit. The mean daily maximum for the year is 60.5 degrees Fahrenheit and the mean daily minimum is 39.3 degrees Fahrenheit. The all-time record high is 110 degrees Fahrenheit, recorded in July, while the all-time record low is negative 33 degrees Fahrenheit, recorded in January. Average annual precipitation totals 38.75 inches, and average annual snowfall is 34.3 inches. The city experiences an average of 117.6 precipitation days and 24.6 snowy days per year.

The 2020 census recorded a population of 41,535, a dramatic 58 percent increase from the 2000 census figure of 26,294. There were 16,885 households and 10,994 families. Population density was 2,333.0 inhabitants per square mile. The city had 17,810 housing units at an average density of 1,000.4 per square mile. The racial makeup was 88.2 percent White, 3.1 percent Black or African American, 0.2 percent Native American, 2.2 percent Asian, 0.9 percent from other races, and 5.4 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino residents of any race comprised 3.1 percent. Among households, 32.5 percent had children under 18, and 50.5 percent were married couples. Non-family households made up 34.9 percent, and 29.4 percent consisted of individuals living alone. The median age was 38.6 years. Residents under 20 constituted 27.6 percent, those aged 25 to 44 made up 26.4 percent, those 45 to 64 accounted for 24.2 percent, and those 65 and older represented 16.9 percent. The gender composition was 48.5 percent male and 51.5 percent female.

The 2010 census showed a population of 34,768 with 14,108 households and 9,308 families. Population density was 2,166.2 per square mile, and there were 15,064 housing units. The racial composition was 94.0 percent White, 2.0 percent African American, 1.3 percent Asian, and 2.0 percent Hispanic or Latino. The average household size was 2.44 and the average family size was 3.02. The median age was 36.1 years.

The 2000 census recorded 26,294 people in 10,458 households and 7,174 families. The city was 97.01 percent White. The median household income was 48,591 dollars and the median family income was 59,110 dollars. Males earned a median of 40,766 dollars versus 26,241 dollars for females. Per capita income was 23,158 dollars. About 3.9 percent of families and 5.2 percent of the total population lived below the poverty line, including 7.2 percent of those under 18 and 5.4 percent of those 65 and older.

The Marion Economic Development Corporation, a public-private partnership founded in 1984, supports business growth in the city. Marion is governed by a mayor; Nicolas AbouAssaly has served as mayor since winning election in 2015 and was re-elected unopposed in 2023 for his third term. Three public school districts serve the city: Marion Independent School District, Linn-Mar Community School District, and Cedar Rapids Community School District. Linn-Mar is one of the fastest-growing school systems in Iowa. Two private schools also operate within the city: St. Joseph School, a Catholic institution founded in 1947 serving prekindergarten through eighth grade with approximately 184 students, and Grace Baptist School, a K-12 institution with about 52 students. Transportation infrastructure includes U.S. Route 151 and Iowa Highway 13 running north to south, Seventh Avenue serving as the major arterial road toward Cedar Rapids, Cedar Rapids Transit Bus Route 20, and a small public airport on the east side. Cultural landmarks include the Granger House Museum, a restored Victorian home listed on the National Register of Historic Places, and the Marion Heritage Center, a former Methodist church from the 1850s now housing historical exhibits and the 1939 fresco mural Communication by Mail by artist Dan Rhodes.
