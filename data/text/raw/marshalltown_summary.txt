Marshalltown is a city in Marshall County, Iowa, United States, and serves as the county seat. The population was 27,591 at the 2020 census and was estimated at 27,886 in 2024, making it the 17th most populous city in Iowa. The city is home to the Iowa Veterans Home and Marshalltown Community College. It is located at coordinates 42 degrees 2 minutes 3 seconds north latitude and 92 degrees 54 minutes 23 seconds west longitude, at an elevation of 899 feet.

Henry Anson was the first European settler in the area, arriving in April 1851. He built a log cabin on a high point between the Iowa River and Linn Creek. In 1853, Anson named the town Marshall after Marshall, Michigan. The name was changed to Marshalltown in 1862 to avoid confusion with another Marshall in Henry County. With help from Potawatomi chief Johnny Green, Anson persuaded early settlers to remain. In the mid-1850s, Anson donated land for a county courthouse, and in 1863 the county seat transferred from the village of Marietta to Marshalltown, driven largely by the location of the Cedar Rapids and Missouri River Railroad. The city was formally incorporated on March 5, 1923. By 1900, the population had reached 10,000, and industries such as Fisher Controls, Lennox International, and Marshalltown Company began developing in the area.

According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 19.193 square miles, of which 19.167 square miles is land and 0.026 square miles is water, representing 0.14 percent water coverage. The climate is classified as hot-summer humid continental under the Koppen system, designated Dfa. Record temperatures range from a high of 112 degrees Fahrenheit in July to a record low of negative 35 degrees Fahrenheit in February. The annual daily mean temperature is 47.6 degrees Fahrenheit. Average annual precipitation is 37.87 inches, and average annual snowfall is 25.3 inches, with approximately 105.1 precipitation days and 15.6 snowy days per year.

The 2020 census recorded 27,591 people, 10,275 households, and 6,588 families. Population density was 1,437.48 inhabitants per square mile. There were 11,248 housing units at an average density of 586.02 per square mile. The racial makeup was 64.81 percent White, 2.67 percent African American, 1.08 percent Native American, 5.33 percent Asian, 0.11 percent Pacific Islander, 14.40 percent from other races, and 11.60 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino residents of any race comprised 31.29 percent of the population. Among households, 33.2 percent had children under 18, and 45.7 percent were married couples. Non-family households made up 35.9 percent of the total, with 30.3 percent consisting of individuals living alone. The median age was 36.7 years. Residents under 20 comprised 29.0 percent, those aged 25 to 44 made up 24.0 percent, and those 65 or older accounted for 18.6 percent. Gender distribution was 50.2 percent male and 49.8 percent female.

The city has experienced significant demographic shifts over the decades. The non-Hispanic White population declined from 96.60 percent in 1990 to 57.60 percent in 2020, while the Hispanic or Latino population grew from 0.98 percent in 1990 to 31.29 percent in 2020. The Asian population also increased notably, from 1.06 percent in 1990 to 5.33 percent in 2020. This diversification was driven largely by immigration from Mexico during the 1990s and 2000s, connected to local meatpacking operations, and a later wave of Burmese refugees in the 2010s.

As of the 2023 American Community Survey, Marshalltown had an estimated 10,127 households with an average of 2.59 persons per household. Median household income was 68,854 dollars, while approximately 14.0 percent of the population lived at or below the poverty line. The employment rate was 63.5 percent, with 19.7 percent holding a bachelor's degree or higher and 82.1 percent holding a high school diploma. The average home price was approximately 169,711 dollars as of late 2025.

The largest employer is JBS Swift and Company, a pork processing facility with 2,700 employees. Other major employers include Emerson Electric Process Management and Fisher Controls with 1,100 employees, the Marshalltown Community School District with 900, Lennox Industries with 750, and the Iowa Veterans Home with 750. Total employment among the top fourteen employers reaches 8,306.

The Marshalltown Community School District operates six elementary schools, one intermediate school, a middle school, and Marshalltown High School, which enrolls over 1,000 students. A Catholic school serves pre-kindergarten through sixth grade, and a Christian school serves grades one through eight. Transportation infrastructure includes U.S. Route 30 bypassing the city to the south, Iowa Highway 14 running through the center, and Iowa Highway 330 providing an expressway connection to Des Moines. The city offers public bus service through Marshalltown Municipal Transit, taxicab services, and intercity coach service via Trailways. A municipal airport is located approximately four miles north, while the closest commercial airport is Des Moines International Airport, 53 miles to the southwest. Marshalltown was struck by an F3 tornado on April 23, 1961, causing one fatality and one million dollars in damage. On July 19, 2018, an EF3 tornado with 145 mile per hour winds moved through downtown, injuring 23 people. A powerful derecho on August 10, 2020, caused extensive damage citywide, leaving nearly 7,000 residents without power for over a week.
