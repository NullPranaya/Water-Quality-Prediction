Mason City is a city in and the county seat of Cerro Gordo County, Iowa, United States. Nicknamed "River City," it recorded a population of 27,338 in the 2020 census, ranking it 16th in the state of Iowa. The city is located at coordinates 43 degrees 8 minutes 55 seconds north latitude and 93 degrees 12 minutes 7 seconds west longitude, at an elevation of 1,125 feet. It serves as a regional commercial, industrial, and cultural hub for north-central Iowa alongside nearby Clear Lake. The Mason City Micropolitan Statistical Area, encompassing all of Cerro Gordo and Worth counties, sits at the transportation junction between Interstate 35 and the Avenue of the Saints. The Winnebago River traverses the community to the southeast.

According to the United States Census Bureau, the city covers a total area of 28.15 square miles, of which 27.86 square miles is land and 0.30 square miles is water. Mason City has a warm-summer humid continental climate classified as Dfb under the Koppen system. The record high temperature is 104 degrees Fahrenheit, recorded in July, while the record low is negative 32 degrees Fahrenheit in February. The annual daily mean temperature is 45.6 degrees Fahrenheit. Average annual precipitation totals 36.25 inches, and average annual snowfall reaches 44.4 inches across an average of 30.2 snowy days per year.

The first white settlers arrived in 1853, including John Long, Joseph Hewitt, and George Brentner from LaSalle, Illinois. Long, a Freemason, initially named the densely wooded area Masonic Grove. The settlement was subsequently known as Shibboleth and Masonville before being renamed Mason City in 1855 to distinguish it from another Masonville in Delaware County. Cerro Gordo County organized its government in August 1855, and Mason City was selected as the county seat. The United States Post Office Department began service to the town in 1857. Mason City was officially incorporated in 1870, with Darius B. Mason serving as the first mayor. Mason City operates under a mayor-council form of government and is currently led by Mayor John Lee.

Industry arrived in 1866 with the completion of the Mason City and Fort Dodge Railroad. The city was further connected by rail in 1869 via the Dakota Branch of the St. Paul and Milwaukee Railroad. The land surrounding Mason City is rich in clay and limestone, which fueled the growth of a major brick, tile, and cement manufacturing industry. By 1912, Mason City shipped the largest freight tonnage in the state and produced more brick, tile, and Portland cement than any city in the world. The Mason City and Clear Lake Railway, founded in 1896, has operated solely as a freight route since 1937 and is now known as the Iowa Traction Railway, the last electric shortline interurban railway in the nation.

The 2020 census recorded 27,338 residents in 12,271 households and 6,838 families. Population density was 981.4 inhabitants per square mile, and there were 13,584 housing units at an average density of 487.7 per square mile. The racial makeup was 87.0 percent White, 2.9 percent Black or African American, 1.5 percent Asian, 0.5 percent Native American, 0.5 percent Pacific Islander, 1.9 percent from other races, and 5.7 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino residents made up 6.7 percent. Of all households, 23.3 percent had children under 18, 40.1 percent were married couples, and 44.3 percent were non-families. The median age was 42.7 years. The 2010 census showed 28,079 residents, while the 2000 census recorded 29,172, indicating a gradual population decline over two decades. In the 2000 census, median household income was 33,852 dollars, median family income was 45,160 dollars, and per capita income was 18,899 dollars. Approximately 7.2 percent of families and 10.0 percent of the population lived below the poverty line.

The largest employer is MercyOne North Iowa Medical Center, which serves 14 counties and opened a 10.6 million dollar behavioral center in June 2019. Other major employers include Curries, Kraft Foods, Cargill Kitchen Solutions, Principal Financial, and a Heidelberg Materials cement plant. The Kraft Foods facility produces the entire national supply of refrigerated Jell-O pudding snacks. Mason City is widely known for its Prairie School architecture, featuring at least 32 structures built between 1908 and 1922. Frank Lloyd Wright designed the Stockman House in 1908 and the Park Inn Hotel and City National Bank buildings in 1909 to 1910. The Park Inn Hotel is the sole surviving hotel designed by Wright. The Rock Crest-Rock Glen Historic District holds the largest collection of Prairie School homes in a natural setting anywhere in the world.

Mason City is the hometown of composer Meredith Willson, whose musical The Music Man was inspired by his childhood there. The Charles H. MacNider Art Museum opened in 1966 and houses American art and Bil Baird puppets. Mason City Municipal Airport provides commercial service through United Airlines and is historically significant as the departure point for Buddy Holly, Ritchie Valens, and The Big Bopper on the night of February 3, 1959, an event later known as the Day the Music Died. Public education is provided by the Mason City Community School District and North Iowa Area Community College. Mason City maintains a sister city relationship with Montegrotto Terme, Italy, established in 2005.
