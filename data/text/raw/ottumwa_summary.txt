Ottumwa is a city in and the county seat of Wapello County, Iowa, United States. Located in the southeastern section of the state, the city is split into northern and southern halves by the Des Moines River. The population was 25,529 at the time of the 2020 census, making it the 18th largest city in Iowa. The city's coordinates are 41 degrees 01 minutes 10 seconds north latitude and 92 degrees 25 minutes 07 seconds west longitude, at an elevation of 650 feet. Its ZIP code is 52501 and its area code is 641. The city operates under a mayor and council form of government, with Rick Johnson serving as mayor.

The city's name derives from the Meskwaki language, translating to "tumbling waters" in reference to the Appanoose Rapids on the Des Moines River. The original town was founded on the former camps of Meskwaki Chiefs Wapello and Appanoose. In May 1843, investors forming the Appanoose Rapids Company staked claim to 467 acres of land at the present site. The original plat was titled Louisville. In 1844, Ottumwa was named the county seat of Wapello County. The town was severely damaged during the flood of 1851. The Burlington and Missouri River Railroad arrived in September 1859, running parallel to the river channel and encouraging commercial and industrial expansion. South Ottumwa was not incorporated into the city limits until the 1880s.

Coal mining shaped much of the city's early economic history. By 1872, Brown and Godfrey employed 300 men and produced 77,000 tons annually from a drift mine northwest of town. By 1889, the state mine inspector's report listed 15 mine shafts in Ottumwa. In 1914, the Phillips Fuel Company produced over 100,000 tons of coal. From 1890 to 1892, the Coal Palace was erected as an exhibition center celebrating the industry. Ottumwa also developed through farm-equipment manufacturing and meatpacking, anchored by John Deere and John Morrell and Company. The Morrell meatpacking complex played a significant role in the city's development from 1877 to 1973. Ottumwa is the center of the Ottumwa Micropolitan Statistical Area, which includes all of Wapello and Davis counties.

According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 16.53 square miles, of which 15.86 square miles is land and 0.67 square miles is water. The climate is classified as hot-summer humid continental under the Koppen system, designated Dfa. Record temperatures range from 106 degrees Fahrenheit in July to negative 27 degrees Fahrenheit in February. The annual daily mean temperature is 50.7 degrees Fahrenheit. Average annual precipitation is 36.97 inches and average annual snowfall is 23.6 inches, with an average of 109.3 precipitation days and 17.1 snowy days per year.

The 2020 census recorded 25,529 people in 10,098 households and 6,048 families. Population density was 1,589.5 per square mile, with 11,254 housing units at an average density of 700.7 per square mile. The racial makeup was 74.3 percent White, 5.4 percent Black or African American, 2.3 percent Asian, 2.0 percent Pacific Islander, 0.8 percent Native American, 8.6 percent from other races, and 6.7 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino residents comprised 15.7 percent. Among households, 30.8 percent had children under 18, 39.6 percent were married couples, and 40.1 percent were non-families. The median age was 36.7 years. The gender split was 49.5 percent male and 50.5 percent female.

The 2010 census showed 25,023 people in 10,251 households and 6,208 families. The racial composition was 90.2 percent White, with Hispanic or Latino at 4.06 percent. The average household size was 2.36 and the average family size was 2.97. The 2000 census recorded 24,998 residents, with the city 95.33 percent White and 2.76 percent Hispanic or Latino. Median household income was 30,174 dollars, median family income was 37,302 dollars, and per capita income was 16,040 dollars. About 10.9 percent of families and 15.2 percent of the population lived below the poverty line.

According to the city's 2023 financial report, the largest employer is JBS Live Pork with 2,550 employees, followed by the Ottumwa Community School District with 800, Ottumwa Regional Health Center with 692, and John Deere Ottumwa Works with 550. Other major employers include Winger Companies, Indian Hills Community College, Hy-Vee, Walmart, the City of Ottumwa, and Keurig Dr Pepper.

The Ottumwa Community School District serves the city and surrounding areas, operating Ottumwa High School, Evans Junior High, and multiple elementary schools. Indian Hills Community College provides higher education, and Buena Vista University maintains a regional campus. In 1982, Ottumwa's mayor proclaimed the city the "Video Game Capital of the World" due to Twin Galaxies, a video game scorekeeping organization based there, and the city hosted the first North American Video Olympics that same year. Transportation infrastructure includes Ottumwa Regional Airport, Amtrak service via the California Zephyr line, BNSF Railway freight service on the major Chicago to Omaha corridor, the Ottumwa Transit Authority bus system with five weekday routes, and highway access via U.S. Route 34 and Iowa Highway 149.
