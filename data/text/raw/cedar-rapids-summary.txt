Cedar Rapids is a city and the county seat of Linn County, Iowa, United States. Known as the "City of Five Seasons," it recorded a population of 137,710 at the 2020 census, with an estimated 137,904 in 2024, making it Iowa's second-most populous city. The city lies on both banks of the Cedar River, approximately 20 miles north of Iowa City and 128 miles northeast of Des Moines. Cedar Rapids serves as the economic hub of Eastern Iowa, located at the core of the Interstate 380 corridor. The three-county Cedar Rapids metropolitan area, which includes the nearby cities of Marion and Hiawatha, had a population of 276,520 in 2020. The metropolitan area is also part of a combined statistical area with the Iowa City metropolitan area.

The location of present-day Cedar Rapids was in the territory of the Meskwaki and Sauk peoples at the time of European American settlement. The first settler on the site was Osgood Shepherd, who built a cabin in 1837 or 1838 next to the Cedar River. The true founders of the city were George Greene and Nicholas Brown, who purchased Shepherd's claim in 1841 and platted a town called Rapids City. Brown built the first mill, and engineer Alexander Ely later constructed a dam creating a mill race capable of powering several mills. The town was formally incorporated by the Iowa State Legislature on January 15, 1849, as Cedar Rapids, named for the rapids in the Cedar River. The population at incorporation was less than 400. During the 1850s, the city grew substantially, and by its reincorporation in 1856, a quarter of its roughly 1,600 inhabitants were Czech immigrants.

According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 76.037 square miles, of which 74.787 square miles is land and 1.250 square miles is water. Cedar Rapids has a humid continental climate with long, cold winters and hot, humid summers. The record low temperature is negative 30 degrees Fahrenheit, set on January 31, 2019, while the record high of 110 degrees Fahrenheit was set on July 6, 1911.

The 2020 census counted 137,710 residents in 58,128 households and 33,116 families. Population density was 1,909.91 inhabitants per square mile. There were 62,398 housing units at an average density of 865.40 per square mile. The racial makeup was 77.79 percent White, 10.41 percent African American, 2.73 percent Asian, 0.35 percent Pacific Islander, 0.29 percent Native American, 1.65 percent from other races, and 6.77 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino persons of any race comprised 4.72 percent of the population.

In the 2010 census, Cedar Rapids had 126,326 residents in 53,236 households. The racial composition was 87.98 percent White, 5.58 percent African American, 2.21 percent Asian, and 3.31 percent Hispanic or Latino. Among households, 28.9 percent had children under 18, 42.8 percent were married couples, and 41.9 percent were non-families. The average household size was 2.31 and the average family size was 2.95. The median age was 35.3 years. The median household income was 51,186 dollars and the median family income was 63,265 dollars. Per capita income was 26,370 dollars. About 6.3 percent of families and 11.7 percent of the population lived below the poverty line.

As of the 2024 American Community Survey, there were an estimated 59,074 households with an average of 2.28 persons per household. The median household income was 69,917 dollars. Approximately 10.5 percent of residents lived at or below the poverty line. The city had a 65.7 percent employment rate, with 32.9 percent holding a bachelor's degree or higher and 93.1 percent holding a high school diploma. The median age was 37.7 years. As of late 2025, the average home price in Cedar Rapids was approximately 202,097 dollars.

Cedar Rapids is one of the largest cities in the world for corn processing. The grain processing industry directly provides 4,000 jobs with average pay of 85,000 dollars and supports an additional 8,000 jobs indirectly. Fortune 500 company Collins Aerospace and trucking company CRST are headquartered in the city, and Aegon maintains its United States headquarters there. A large Quaker Oats mill dominates the north side of downtown. Other major companies with facilities in Cedar Rapids include Archer Daniels Midland, Cargill, General Mills, Toyota Financial Services, and Nordstrom. In February 2025, a 750 million dollar data center project by Quality Technology Services was announced as the largest economic development in the city's history.

The city has faced significant natural disasters. During the Iowa flood of 2008, the Cedar River reached a record 31.12 feet, flooding 1,126 city blocks and forcing evacuation of 7,749 properties, causing billions of dollars in damages. In August 2020, an intense derecho struck with estimated peak gusts of 140 miles per hour, damaging the majority of buildings and leaving 95 percent of the city without power.

Cedar Rapids has over 3,360 acres of city-owned green space and 74 formally named parks or recreational facilities, with 24 miles of trail systems. The Mother Mosque of America, dedicated in 1934, is the longest-standing mosque in North America and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places. The city is home to the Cedar Rapids Museum of Art, which houses the largest collection of Grant Wood paintings in the world, along with the National Czech and Slovak Museum and Library. Professional sports teams include the Cedar Rapids Kernels of minor league baseball and the Cedar Rapids RoughRiders of the United States Hockey League. From 1908 to 2005, Cedar Rapids used a city commission form of government before transitioning to its current structure.
