Davenport is a city and the county seat of Scott County, Iowa, United States. The population was 101,724 at the 2020 census, with an estimated 100,938 in 2024, making it Iowa's third-most populous city after Des Moines and Cedar Rapids. The city is situated along the Mississippi River on the eastern border of the state. Together with Bettendorf in Iowa and Rock Island, Moline, and East Moline in Illinois, Davenport is part of the Quad Cities, the largest city in that bi-state region. The Quad Cities metropolitan area had a population of 384,324 in 2020, while the combined statistical area population was 474,019.

Davenport was founded on May 14, 1836, by Antoine Le Claire and named for his friend Colonel George Davenport, who was stationed at Fort Armstrong. Le Claire, a mixed-race man of French-Canadian and Native American descent, had served as translator during the Black Hawk Purchase of 1832, which acquired approximately 6 million acres of land in what is now eastern Iowa for 640,000 dollars. The city was formally incorporated on January 25, 1839. The land was originally inhabited by the Sauk, Meskwaki, and Ho-Chunk peoples. Scott County was formed in 1837 by the Wisconsin Territorial legislature, and Davenport became the county seat after winning three contested elections against the rival town of Rockingham.

According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 66.333 square miles, of which 64.209 square miles is land and 2.124 square miles is water, representing 3.2 percent. Davenport is located approximately 170 miles west of Chicago and 170 miles east of Des Moines, the state capital. The Mississippi River at Davenport has a maximum depth of 30 to 40 feet and is 2,217 feet wide where the Centennial Bridge crosses it. Notably, the river flows from east to west in this area rather than its usual north-to-south direction.

Davenport has a humid continental climate under the Koppen classification, subtype Dfa. Summers are warm to hot with high humidity, while winters are cold with average annual snowfall of 30.7 inches. The record high temperature of 111 degrees Fahrenheit was set on July 12, 1936, and the record low of negative 29 degrees Fahrenheit was recorded on January 18, 2009.

The 2020 census recorded 101,724 residents in 42,679 households and 24,669 families. Population density was 1,594.55 inhabitants per square mile. There were 46,964 housing units at an average density of 736.17 per square mile. The racial makeup was 74.06 percent White, 11.98 percent African American, 2.20 percent Asian, 0.43 percent Native American, 0.04 percent Pacific Islander, 2.57 percent from other races, and 8.73 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino persons of any race comprised 8.83 percent.

In the 2010 census, Davenport had 99,685 residents. The racial composition was 80.66 percent White, 10.79 percent African American, 2.18 percent Asian, and 7.28 percent Hispanic or Latino. In the 2000 census, the population was 98,359. The median household income was 37,242 dollars, the median family income was 45,944 dollars, and the per capita income was 18,828 dollars. About 10.5 percent of families and 14.1 percent of the population were below the poverty line.

As of the 2024 American Community Survey, there were an estimated 42,463 households with an average of 2.30 persons per household. The median household income had risen to 64,686 dollars. Approximately 14.9 percent of the population lived at or below the poverty line. The employment rate was 63.1 percent, with 29.8 percent of residents holding a bachelor's degree or higher and 91.0 percent holding a high school diploma. The median age was 37.3 years. The average home price as of late 2025 was approximately 179,841 dollars.

From 1860 until 1980, Davenport enjoyed a long period of industrial growth, averaging roughly 760 new residents per year. Industries included locomotive manufacturing, meatpacking, and heavy machinery. Since 1980, population growth has been essentially flat at around 100,000, as major industries departed. The farm crisis of the 1980s resulted in approximately 35,000 job losses across the entire Quad Cities area. Manufacturing remains the city's biggest labor sector with over 7,600 jobs. Major employers include Genesis Health System with approximately 4,900 employees in Davenport, the Davenport Community School District with 2,237 employees, and a John Deere plant on the north side. Davenport is also the headquarters of department store Von Maur and newspaper publisher Lee Enterprises.

Davenport is the largest city bordering the Mississippi River without a permanent flood wall or levee. The city's existing flood wall dates from 1919. During the Great Flood of 1993, the river crested at 22.63 feet, nearly 8 feet above the 14.9-foot flood stage. Flooding also occurred in 2008, 2019, and 2023.

The city has two main universities: St. Ambrose University and Palmer College of Chiropractic, where the first chiropractic adjustment took place. Davenport has over 50 parks and more than 20 miles of recreational paths. Notable cultural attractions include the Figge Art Museum, the Putnam Museum founded in 1867, and the German American Heritage Center. The annual Bix 7 road race attracts 12,000 to 18,000 runners. The Quad Cities River Bandits minor league baseball team plays at Modern Woodmen Park in downtown Davenport. Three interstate highways serve the city: I-80, I-74, and I-280. The first railroad bridge across the Mississippi River was built at Davenport in 1856, connecting it to Rock Island, Illinois, and significantly boosting regional commerce.
