Johnston is a city in Polk County, Iowa, United States, located within the Webster and Jefferson townships. Its motto is "Thrive. Every Day." The city is part of the Des Moines-West Des Moines Metropolitan Statistical Area and is situated just north of Iowa's capital. Johnston's coordinates are 41 degrees 41 minutes 28 seconds north latitude and 93 degrees 43 minutes 25 seconds west longitude, at an elevation of 824 feet. The city was incorporated on September 18, 1969, and operates under a mayor-council form of government. Its ZIP code is 50131, and its area code is 515.

Johnston traces its origins to 1905, when it was established as a station on the Des Moines and Central Iowa Railway, a line running between Des Moines and Perry. The station was called Johnston Station, named after the railway's freight supervisor, John F. Johnston. Passenger service along the railroad ended in 1949, and the tracks have since been removed within city limits. Voters passed a resolution to incorporate on June 23, 1969, and the first mayor and council were sworn in on September 8 of that year. Incorporation papers were officially signed by the Clerk of the District Court on December 11, 1969. At the time of incorporation, the Johnston Station area was excluded from the new city due to a state law preventing a town within three miles of another city from incorporating. Both Des Moines and Urbandale had interests in that land, but in April 1970, Johnston and Urbandale reached an agreement that allowed Johnston to annex the Johnston Station area.

According to the United States Census Bureau, Johnston has a total area of 18.37 square miles, of which 17.16 square miles is land and 1.21 square miles is water. However, more recent data lists the total area as 20.88 square miles, with 19.39 square miles of land and 1.48 square miles of water. The city is bordered on the east by the Des Moines River, and its area includes part of Saylorville Reservoir. Interstate 80 forms the southern boundary, while the western city limits extend to a half mile east of Iowa Highway 141. Beaver Creek bisects the city, flowing southward until it nears Interstate 80, where it turns eastward to join the Des Moines River.

The 2020 census recorded a population of 24,064, ranking Johnston 24th among Iowa cities. This represents dramatic growth from 8,649 residents in 2000. The population density was 1,240.9 inhabitants per square mile. There were 9,896 housing units at an average density of 510.3 per square mile, with 9,194 occupied households and 6,328 families. The racial makeup was 81.7 percent White, 6.3 percent Asian, 5.9 percent Black or African American, 0.2 percent Native American, 1.3 percent from other races, and 4.7 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino residents of any race comprised 3.9 percent of the population. Among households, 36.5 percent had children under 18, and 57.0 percent were married couples living together. Non-family households made up 31.2 percent of the total, with 25.2 percent consisting of individuals and 10.4 percent being individuals aged 65 or older living alone. The median age was 37.0 years. Residents under 20 accounted for 29.8 percent, those aged 25 to 44 made up 25.2 percent, those 45 to 64 comprised 25.1 percent, and those 65 or older represented 13.8 percent. The gender split was 48.4 percent male and 51.6 percent female.

The 2010 census counted 17,278 residents in 6,369 households and 4,720 families. Population density was 1,006.9 per square mile. The racial composition was 91.0 percent White, 4.6 percent Asian, 2.2 percent African American, and 2.0 percent Hispanic or Latino. The average household size was 2.67 and the average family size was 3.14. The median age was 36.9 years, with 29.9 percent of residents under 18 and 9.9 percent aged 65 or older.

The 2000 census recorded 8,649 people in 3,216 households and 2,318 families. Population density was 602.8 per square mile. The city was 96.20 percent White, 2.00 percent Asian, 0.58 percent African American, and 1.53 percent Hispanic or Latino. The average household size was 2.61 and the average family size was 3.13. The median age was 36 years. The median household income was 76,094 dollars and the median family income was 97,322 dollars. Males earned a median income of 61,585 dollars compared to 36,008 dollars for females. Per capita income was 36,407 dollars. About 2.0 percent of families and 4.1 percent of the population lived below the poverty line, including 2.4 percent of those under 18 and 10.8 percent of those 65 and older.

Johnston is home to several prominent institutions and employers. Pioneer Hi-Bred Seeds, Corteva Agriscience, and John Deere Financial are all headquartered in the city. Iowa Public Television and The Gardeners of America also maintain their headquarters here. The Camp Dodge Military Reservation is located within the city, as is the Des Moines Weather Forecast Office of the National Weather Service. The Paul J. and Ida Trier House, designed by architect Frank Lloyd Wright, is a notable private residence in the area. The Johnston Community School District serves the city's educational needs. Public transit is provided by Des Moines Area Regional Transit, with Routes 5 and 93 offering bus service connecting Johnston to the broader region.
