Waukee is a city in Dallas County, Iowa, United States, and is part of the Des Moines metropolitan area. Its motto is "The Key To Good Living." The city is located at coordinates 41 degrees 35 minutes 26 seconds north latitude and 93 degrees 52 minutes 58 seconds west longitude, at an elevation of 1,047 feet. According to the 2020 census, the population was 23,940, and by 2024 it was estimated at 34,420, making Waukee the 16th most populous city in Iowa. The Des Moines metropolitan area as a whole had a population of 753,913, with an urban population of 542,486.

Waukee was laid out as a town in 1869. The name is believed to derive from the Milwaukee Road Railroad. The city was incorporated on December 23, 1878. General Lewis Addison Grant and Major William Ragan originally purchased the land that would become Waukee. The Des Moines Valley Railroad was built through the area, spurring early commercial development. Waukee was primarily a coal mining community until 1949, when the Shuler Mine, Iowa's largest coal producer at the time, closed.

According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 22.002 square miles, of which 21.907 square miles is land and 0.095 square miles is water, with water comprising 0.43 percent of the total area. The climate is classified as humid continental under the Koppen classification system, designated "Dfa," which is characterized by large seasonal temperature differences with hot, humid summers and cold winters. The ZIP code is 50263 and the area code is 515.

Waukee has experienced dramatic population growth across recent decades. In 1990, the population was 2,512. By 2000, it had grown to 5,126, more than doubling in a decade. The 2010 census recorded 13,790 residents, representing an increase of nearly 170 percent. The 2020 count of 23,940 marked continued rapid expansion, and the 2024 estimate of 34,420 indicates that growth has not slowed. The population density as of the 2020 census was 1,093 inhabitants per square mile.

The 2020 census recorded 23,940 people, 9,107 households, and 6,208 families in Waukee. There were 9,831 housing units at an average density of 472.67 per square mile. The racial composition was 83.13 percent White, 5.30 percent Asian, 3.73 percent African American, 0.25 percent Native American, 0.07 percent Pacific Islander, 1.53 percent from other races, and 5.99 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino residents of any race made up 5.23 percent. Among households, 40.2 percent had children under 18, and 55.0 percent were married couples. Non-family households comprised 31.8 percent of the total, with 25.3 percent consisting of individuals living alone. The median age was 33.9 years. Residents under 20 accounted for 31.0 percent, those aged 25 to 44 made up 32.5 percent, and those 65 or older comprised 11.1 percent. The gender breakdown was 48.5 percent male and 51.5 percent female.

Racial and ethnic data over time show increasing diversity. In 1990, the city was 99.36 percent non-Hispanic White. By 2000, that figure was 97.66 percent. It declined to 91.96 percent by 2010 and further to 81.68 percent by 2020. The non-Hispanic Black population grew from zero in 1990 to 890 in 2020. The Asian population rose from 7 in 1990 to 1,264 in 2020. Hispanic or Latino residents increased from 8 in 1990 to 1,253 in 2020.

The 2010 census recorded 13,790 residents in 5,154 households and 3,689 families, with 5,378 housing units. The average household size was 2.67 and the average family size was 3.19. The median age was 31.8 years. In 2000, there were 5,126 people in 1,927 households with 2,087 housing units. The median household income was 58,024 dollars, the median family income was 64,362 dollars, and the per capita income was 24,351 dollars. About 2.1 percent of families and 3.0 percent of the population lived below the poverty line.

According to the 2023 American Community Survey, Waukee had 10,652 estimated households with an average of 2.52 persons per household. The median household income was 106,728 dollars, a substantial increase over earlier figures. The poverty rate was 7.3 percent. The employment rate was 75.0 percent. Educational attainment was high, with 56.7 percent holding a bachelor's degree or higher and 95.9 percent holding a high school diploma. There were 11,023 housing units at an average density of 503.17 per square mile. The average home price as of late 2025 was 336,914 dollars. The median age was 34.4 years. The most commonly spoken language was English at 85.0 percent, followed by Indo-European languages at 5.2 percent, Asian and Pacific Islander languages at 3.7 percent, Spanish at 2.9 percent, and other languages at 3.2 percent.

In 2017, Apple Inc. selected Waukee for a 1.38 billion dollar data center campus, a significant economic development milestone. The Waukee Community School District is the fastest-growing district in Iowa, employing more than 2,200 staff and serving over 13,600 students across ten elementary schools, four middle schools, and two high schools, including Waukee High School and Waukee Northwest High School, which opened in 2021. The district covers 55 square miles and serves students from Clive, Urbandale, Waukee, and West Des Moines. Waukee operates under a mayor and city council government led by Mayor Courtney Clarke. HIRTA provides demand-response transit, though as of 2023 Waukee remains the largest city in Iowa without fixed-route public transit. Notable residents include Slipknot drummer Joey Jordison and landscape architect Ken Smith.
