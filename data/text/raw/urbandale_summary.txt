Urbandale is a city spanning Polk and Dallas counties in the state of Iowa, United States. It is situated in the northwest part of the Des Moines metropolitan area and is part of the Des Moines-West Des Moines Metropolitan Statistical Area. As of the 2020 census, the city had a population of 45,580, making it the 12th largest city in Iowa. The city operates under a council-manager form of government, with a city council composed of the mayor and five council members elected to four-year terms with no term limits. The current mayor is Bob Andeweg.

Urbandale was incorporated on April 16, 1917. Before incorporation, citizens raised 2,000 dollars to build a two-room schoolhouse, which was ready by 1916. The push for incorporation was motivated by the desire to establish a separate school district rather than be absorbed into the Johnston School District. In its early years, Urbandale functioned as a streetcar suburb of Des Moines, with four coal mines providing local employment. The streetcar line, originally planned as a railroad extending to Woodward, was terminated at its Urbandale endpoint due to right-of-way disputes. At peak service, the line carried 4,900 passengers in a single day, using 10 cars on a 15.2-mile round trip. The coal mines closed by the end of the 1940s, and streetcar service ended in 1951, replaced by gas shuttle buses. Many current DART bus routes follow the old streetcar paths. In 1956, The Suburban Press launched Urban News, which remains the only Urbandale-specific news publication in the city's history, reaching 800 subscribers by summer 1956 and over 2,500 by 1957.

The city's population growth has been dramatic. In 1920, shortly after incorporation, Urbandale had just 298 residents. By 1950, the population was 1,777. Rapid suburbanization in the postwar era pushed the count to 14,434 by 1970, and it reached 29,072 in 2000. Dallas County, into which Urbandale has expanded westward, has been the fastest growing county in Iowa, with its population increasing more than 50 percent from 2010 to 2020. In 2019, Dallas County was the 8th fastest growing county in the entire United States, with a 36 percent population increase.

The city's geographic coordinates are 41 degrees 37 minutes 51 seconds north latitude and 93 degrees 45 minutes 15 seconds west longitude, at an elevation of 981 feet. According to the United States Census Bureau, Urbandale has a total area of 21.94 square miles, of which 21.92 square miles is land and 0.02 square miles is water. The city sits at the intersection of Interstates 35 and 80, Iowa Highway 141, and U.S. Route 6. In January 2022, Urbandale and the city of Grimes established an annexation boundary agreement governing expansion over the following decade. In June 2025, the city approved annexation of 100 acres previously belonging to Grimes. Urbandale lies within Tornado Alley and has been struck by two tornadoes: an F2 on October 16, 1966, and an EF1 on July 15, 2024.

Urbandale has a humid continental climate classified as Dfa under the Koppen system. The coldest recorded temperature was negative 35.6 degrees Celsius on January 12, 1912, and the hottest was 45.0 degrees Celsius on August 8, 1934. Average annual precipitation is 36.55 inches and average annual snowfall is 36.5 inches.

The 2020 census counted 45,580 people in 17,528 households and 12,398 families. Population density was 2,023.3 inhabitants per square mile. There were 18,366 housing units at an average density of 815.3 per square mile. The racial makeup was 83.1 percent White, 5.5 percent Asian, 4.0 percent Black or African American, 0.2 percent Native American, 1.6 percent from other races, and 5.6 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino residents comprised 4.9 percent. Among households, 35.0 percent had children under 18, and 59.2 percent were married couples. The median age was 39.3 years. The gender composition was 49.2 percent male and 50.8 percent female.

The 2010 census recorded 39,463 people in 15,596 households and 10,815 families. Population density was 1,800.3 per square mile. The racial makeup was 91.1 percent White, 3.5 percent Asian, 2.8 percent African American, and 3.1 percent Hispanic or Latino. The average household size was 2.52 and the average family size was 3.04. The median age was 37.8 years.

Economically, the largest industry is Finance and Insurance at 17.9 percent of the workforce, followed by Healthcare and Social Assistance at 11.8 percent and Retail at 10.5 percent. Top employers include Marsh and McLennan Companies with 1,000 to 1,100 employees, followed by the Urbandale Community School District and Quality Manufacturing Corporation, each with 700 to 800 employees. MidAmerican Energy and the U.S. Postal Service each employ 500 to 700 people. The Multi-State Lottery Association, which oversees Powerball operations, is headquartered in Urbandale. The Urban Loop, a development area along a 12-mile stretch of Interstate 35 and 80, has fueled significant commercial growth. A 65 million dollar flyover ramp connecting the interstates to Highway 141 opened in October 2020.

Urbandale features 850 acres of parks and 54 miles of recreational trails. Living History Farms, where Pope John Paul II addressed an estimated 350,000 people on October 4, 1979, is located in the city. The Buccaneer Arena, home to the Des Moines Buccaneers hockey team, is also in Urbandale. Six public school districts serve portions of the city. The Urbandale Community School District operates six elementary schools, one middle school, and Urbandale High School. Private schools include Des Moines Christian School, with over 1,500 students, and St. Pius X Catholic School.
