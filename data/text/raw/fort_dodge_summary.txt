Fort Dodge is a city and the county seat of Webster County, Iowa, United States, situated along the Des Moines River. Its coordinates are 42 degrees 30 minutes 09 seconds north latitude and 94 degrees 10 minutes 15 seconds west longitude, at an elevation of 1,112 feet. The city's motto is "Frontier of the Future," and it serves as a major commercial center for north-central and northwest Iowa. It is located along U.S. Routes 20 and 169. The population was 24,871 at the 2020 census, a decrease from 25,136 recorded in 2000.

Fort Dodge traces its origins to 1850, when Captain Samuel Woods and E Company of the 6th Infantry were dispatched from Fort Snelling to build a garrison at the junction of the Des Moines River and Lizard Creek, arriving on August 2, 1850. The post was originally named Fort Clarke but was renamed Fort Dodge after it was discovered that another fort in Texas shared the same name. The new name honored Henry Dodge, a governor of Wisconsin Territory, which had included Iowa before statehood in 1846. The Army abandoned the fort in 1853, and the following year civilian storekeeper William Williams purchased the land and buildings. The town of Fort Dodge was officially incorporated in 1869. In 1872, the gypsum industry began when George Ringland, Webb Vincent, and Stillman T. Meservey formed the Fort Dodge Plaster Mills, constructing the first gypsum mill west of the Mississippi River. The city is locally referred to as "Little Chicago" because architects modeled its downtown buildings to resemble Chicago architecture during the mid-1900s.

According to the United States Census Bureau, Fort Dodge has a total area of 16.31 square miles, of which 16.05 square miles is land and 0.26 square miles is water. The city has a humid continental climate with cold winters and hot, humid summers. Climate records dating from 1899 to the present show a record high of 110 degrees Fahrenheit in July and a record low of negative 35 degrees Fahrenheit in January. The annual daily mean temperature is 46.5 degrees Fahrenheit. Average annual precipitation is 35.09 inches, and average annual snowfall is 39.0 inches, spread across an average of 21.3 snowy days per year.

The 2020 census counted 24,871 residents in 9,946 households and 5,428 families. Population density was 1,553.7 inhabitants per square mile. There were 11,298 housing units at an average density of 705.8 per square mile. The racial composition was 83.3 percent White, 7.0 percent Black or African American, 0.6 percent Native American, 1.0 percent Asian, 0.1 percent Pacific Islander, 2.2 percent from other races, and 5.8 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino residents of any race made up 6.8 percent of the population. Among households, 25.3 percent had children under 18, and 36.1 percent were married couples. Non-family households accounted for 45.4 percent of the total. The median age was 38.3 years. The gender distribution was 51.8 percent male and 48.2 percent female.

At the 2010 census, the population was 25,206, with 10,275 households and 5,850 families. The racial makeup was 90 percent White, 5.5 percent African American, 0.4 percent Native American, and 0.8 percent Asian. Hispanic or Latino residents comprised 5.0 percent. The average household size was 2.21, and the average family size was 2.89. The median age was 36.8 years. The 2000 census recorded 25,136 residents and 10,470 households. The city was 92.47 percent White, with Hispanic or Latino residents at 2.94 percent. Median household income was 33,361 dollars, and median family income was 42,555 dollars. Per capita income was 18,018 dollars. About 7.7 percent of families and 11.6 percent of the population lived below the poverty line.

The economy of Fort Dodge centers on biofuels, livestock feed, gypsum and limestone mining, can production, drywall manufacturing, trucking, veterinary pharmaceuticals and vaccines, and retail. Gypsum rock is processed into drywall and plaster at several local facilities operated by National Gypsum Company, Georgia Pacific Corporation, CertainTeed Corporation, and United States Gypsum Company. Drywall was patented by a Fort Dodge resident, and the gypsum used in the famous Cardiff Giant hoax was mined locally. Elanco, formerly part of Boehringer Ingelheim, maintains two of its three United States manufacturing plants in the city for veterinary products. In 2022, Cargill announced plans to build a corn syrup refinery in Fort Dodge. At least three major national trucking companies are headquartered in the city. For much of the twentieth century, meatpacking was a significant industry, but the last large plants closed during the 1980s. The Fort Dodge Correctional Facility, a 1,250-bed medium-security state prison, opened in 1998.

Fort Dodge is home to Iowa Central Community College. The Fort Dodge Community School District operates the public schools, including Fort Dodge Senior High School, Fort Dodge Middle School, and several elementary schools. Private schools include St. Edmond, Community Christian School, St. Paul Lutheran, and Harvest Baptist School. Cultural attractions include the Fort Museum and Frontier Village, which houses a replica of the Cardiff Giant, and the Blanden Memorial Art Museum, the first public art museum in Iowa, which opened on June 5, 1932. Fort Dodge has been named the "Live Music Capital of Iowa" and supports numerous music organizations. Fort Dodge Senior High School holds the distinction of producing the longest-running high school musical theater tradition in the United States, dating back to 1927. Local transit is provided by Dodger Area Rapid Transit, and the Fort Dodge Regional Airport serves general aviation needs north of the city.
