Ankeny is a city in Polk County, Iowa, United States, and a suburb of the state capital of Des Moines. It is part of the Des Moines-West Des Moines Metropolitan Statistical Area and ranks as one of Iowa's fastest-growing cities. The population was 67,887 at the 2020 census and was estimated at 76,727 in 2024, making it the state's fifth-most populous city. The city is located at coordinates 41 degrees 43 minutes 53 seconds north latitude and 93 degrees 36 minutes 4 seconds west longitude, at an elevation of 988 feet. Its motto is "Bringing it all together."

Ankeny was founded as an agrarian community on April 22, 1875, by John Fletcher Ankeny and Sarah Ankeny on 80 acres of land purchased on July 11, 1874, for 1,600 dollars. The town, initially a single square mile, was formally incorporated on February 28, 1903. A railroad linking Des Moines to Ames through Ankeny was completed in 1874, with passenger service established in 1880. In 1881, the town consisted of just seven houses, a church, a depot, a store and post office, a livery stable, a blacksmith shop, a rooming house, a machine shop, and a school. Coal mining became significant in the early twentieth century, with the Enterprise Coal Company producing over 100,000 tons of coal in 1914. In 1941, the federal government seized farmland for a military ordnance plant, which operated from 1942 until 1945. Deere and Company purchased the factory in 1947 and began manufacturing agricultural equipment as John Deere Des Moines Works. The city's status was upgraded from town to city in 1961. An F4 tornado struck Ankeny in June 1974, killing two people and injuring 50. Ankeny Regional Airport opened in 1994.

According to the United States Census Bureau, Ankeny covers a total area of 32.835 square miles, of which 32.655 square miles is land and 0.180 square miles is water, representing 0.55 percent of the total area. The city sits within the Des Moines Lobe, a topographical landscape formed during the Wisconsin glaciation. Ankeny has a hot-summer humid continental climate classified as Dfa under the Koppen system. Record temperatures range from a high of 106 degrees Fahrenheit in August to a low of negative 34 degrees in February. Average annual precipitation is 37.05 inches and average annual snowfall is 24.7 inches.

The 2020 census recorded 67,887 residents in 26,375 households and 17,475 families. Population density was 2,243.68 inhabitants per square mile. There were 27,971 housing units at an average density of 924.45 per square mile. The racial makeup was 87.86 percent White, 2.51 percent African American, 0.22 percent Native American, 2.41 percent Asian, 0.05 percent Pacific Islander, 1.48 percent from other races, and 5.46 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino residents comprised 4.17 percent. The city has grown continuously since its first census in 1910, when the population was just 445. Growth has been particularly rapid in recent decades, rising from 27,117 in 2000 to 45,582 in 2010 and 67,887 in 2020. Ankeny was listed as the fourth fastest-growing city in 2016 to 2017 with a 6.4 percent growth rate.

As of the 2024 American Community Survey, Ankeny had 28,926 households with an average of 2.62 persons per household and a median household income of 102,518 dollars. About 3.4 percent of the population lives at or below the poverty line. The employment rate was 73.8 percent, with 50.7 percent holding a bachelor's degree or higher and 96.1 percent holding a high school diploma. The median age was 33.6 years. As of late 2025, the average home price was 334,054 dollars according to Zillow.

The city's fiscal year 2020 budget listed total property values of 779 million dollars in commercial, 177 million in industrial, 56 million in utilities, 113 million in multi-residential, and 4.64 billion in residential property. There were 5,089 businesses with 972 million dollars in taxable retail sales. The largest employers included Ankeny Community Schools with 1,965 employees, Deere and Company with 1,900, and Casey's General Stores with 1,260. Education and health care services represented the largest employment sector at 23.34 percent.

Ankeny operates under a council-manager form of government with a mayor and five council members elected to four-year terms. The acting mayor is Bobbi Bentz. The Ankeny Community School District is Iowa's sixth largest, with a yearly budget of about 190 million dollars, educating nearly 12,000 students across 16 buildings including two high schools. Private schools include Ankeny Christian Academy and St. Luke the Evangelist Catholic School. Des Moines Area Community College has its main Ankeny Campus on a 304-acre plot educating over 15,000 students. Faith Baptist Bible College and Theological Seminary has operated in the city since 1967. The city maintains 60 parks covering over 800 acres, more than 100 miles of trails, two aquatic centers, and two sports complexes. Infrastructure includes 543 miles of road, 369 cul-de-sacs, 6,238 street lights, and 72 traffic signals.
