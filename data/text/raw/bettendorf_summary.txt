Bettendorf is a city in Scott County, Iowa, United States. Its motto is "A Premier City," and it is one of the five cities that make up the Quad Cities metropolitan region along the Mississippi River. The city is adjacent to Davenport, Iowa, and sits across the river from Moline, East Moline, and Rock Island in Illinois. The broader Quad Cities metropolitan area had a population of 384,324 as of the 2020 census.

The land where Bettendorf now stands was part of the original Wisconsin Territory, acquired from the Sac and Fox Indians through the Black Hawk Purchase of 1832 after the Black Hawk War. The first European-American settlers established a village called Lilienthal, named after a local tavern and dance hall. A neighboring village called Gilbert developed alongside it in 1858, named after Elias Gilbert, who platted the original site. The early residents were predominantly German immigrants who worked as farmers, laborers, and small business owners. The two villages eventually merged into the town of Gilbert. Around 1900, the town granted William and Joseph Bettendorf 70 acres of riverfront land in exchange for relocating their iron wagon business from Davenport. In 1903, the town's 440 citizens petitioned for incorporation and requested the name be changed to Bettendorf in honor of the brothers, whose factory drove the city's early economic development. William Bettendorf was an industrialist and inventor who lived from 1857 to 1910, while Joseph Bettendorf was a businessman who lived from 1864 to 1933. Together they co-founded the Bettendorf Axle Company.

In the late 1940s, the Aluminum Company of America chose Riverdale, an enclave of Bettendorf, as the site for the world's largest aluminum mill. This facility created thousands of jobs and spurred significant population growth that has continued to the present day. Another landmark moment came on April 1, 1991, when the first modern riverboat casinos in the United States launched in Bettendorf. Local businessman Bernard Goldstein, who lived from 1929 to 2009, spearheaded this venture and later founded Isle of Capri Casinos. Goldstein and his family also operate Alter Companies, a scrap metal, barge, and towboat business on the river waterfront. The Quad Cities Waterfront Convention Center opened near the casino and hotel in 2009 and is owned by the city. Bettendorf is also where Ronald Medd invented and patented the Dairy Queen Blizzard machine in 1984.

According to the United States Census Bureau, Bettendorf has a total area of 22.43 square miles, of which 21.3 square miles is land and 1.13 square miles is water. The city lies along the banks of the Mississippi River, making stormwater management a persistent challenge. In 2000, a comprehensive stormwater study identified 169 insufficient inlets unable to handle rainfall exceeding 1.25 inches, with several creeks including Crow Creek, Pigeon Creek, and Spencer Creek contributing to flooding and property damage. The city collects stormwater fees through water bills to fund improvements. Climate data shows record high temperatures reaching 103 degrees Fahrenheit in August, while record lows drop to negative 28 degrees Fahrenheit in February. The mean daily maximum temperature for the year is 60 degrees Fahrenheit, and the mean daily minimum is 42 degrees Fahrenheit. Average annual precipitation totals approximately 866 millimeters.

The 2020 census recorded a population of 39,102 in Bettendorf. Population density was 1,835.6 inhabitants per square mile. There were 16,697 housing units at an average density of 783.8 per square mile. The racial makeup was 82.4 percent White, 6.2 percent Asian, 3.7 percent Black or African American, 0.2 percent Native American, 1.3 percent from other races, and 6.2 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino residents of any race comprised 5.3 percent of the population.

At the 2010 census, the population was 33,217, with 13,681 households and 9,225 families. Population density was 1,565.4 per square mile, and there were 14,437 housing units. The racial composition was 91.9 percent White, 3.1 percent Asian, 2.2 percent African American, and 3.6 percent Hispanic or Latino. Of all households, 33.0 percent had children under 18, 55.7 percent were married couples, and 32.6 percent were non-families. The average household size was 2.42 and the average family size was 2.97. The median age was 40.7 years. Residents under 18 made up 25.5 percent, while 14.8 percent were 65 or older. The gender breakdown was 48.6 percent male and 51.4 percent female.

Bettendorf operates under a mayor and city council form of government with seven council members, two at-large and five representing individual wards. Two public school districts serve the city: the Bettendorf Community School District, covering central, northern, and western areas with five elementary schools, one middle school, and one high school; and the Pleasant Valley Community School District, covering eastern Bettendorf and nearby communities. Three private schools also operate within city limits: Rivermont Collegiate, Lourdes Catholic School, and Morning Star Academy. Edison Academy serves as a consortium alternative high school. Scott Community College and Upper Iowa University both maintain a presence in the area. A major infrastructure project, the 1.2 billion dollar I-74 Bridge replacement connecting Bettendorf to Moline, began construction in July 2017 and was completed in 2021, spurring new downtown commercial and residential development.
