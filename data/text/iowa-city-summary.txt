Iowa City is the largest city in Johnson County, Iowa, United States, and serves as the county seat. Nicknamed the "Athens of Iowa," it recorded a population of 74,828 in the 2020 census, making it Iowa's fifth-most populous city. The Iowa City metropolitan area, encompassing Johnson and Washington counties, has a population exceeding 171,000. Combined with the Cedar Rapids metropolitan area, the broader region has a population of nearly 500,000.

Iowa City was created by an act of the Legislative Assembly of the Iowa Territory on January 21, 1839, to serve as a new territorial capital closer to the center of the territory. Commissioners selected a site on bluffs above the Iowa River, and the town was platted and surveyed by June of that year. Construction of the Old Capitol Building began in 1839, and the city officially became the territorial capital in 1841. Iowa City served as the third and final territorial capital from 1841 to 1846 and as the first state capital after Iowa achieved statehood on December 28, 1846, as the 29th state. The state capital was relocated to Des Moines in 1857. The Old Capitol Building is now a National Historic Landmark and museum on the University of Iowa campus.

Iowa City is located in eastern Iowa along the Iowa River, on Interstate 80, approximately 60 miles west of the Quad Cities. According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 25.28 square miles, of which 25.01 square miles is land and 0.27 square miles is water. The elevation at the Iowa City Municipal Airport is 668 feet above sea level. The city is contiguous with Coralville to the northwest, and University Heights is completely contained within its boundaries.

The climate is humid continental, classified as Koppen subtype Dfa. Average monthly temperatures range from 22.8 degrees Fahrenheit in January to 75.8 degrees Fahrenheit in July. The annual daily mean temperature is 48.9 degrees Fahrenheit. The record high is 109 degrees Fahrenheit and the record low is negative 32 degrees Fahrenheit. Average annual precipitation totals approximately 977 millimeters, with June being the wettest month. Snowfall is moderate in winter, and severe thunderstorms and tornadoes are possible from May through September.

The 2020 census counted 74,828 residents in 30,291 households and 13,206 families. Population density was 2,923.3 inhabitants per square mile. There were 32,854 housing units at an average density of 1,283.5 per square mile. The racial composition was 72.5 percent White, 10.2 percent Black or African American, 7.3 percent Asian, 0.2 percent Native American, 3.0 percent from other races, and 6.7 percent from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino persons of any race comprised 7.3 percent. The median age was 27.1 years, reflecting the large university student population. Among residents, 21.0 percent were under 20, 24.3 percent were aged 20 to 24, 27.1 percent were 25 to 44, 16.1 percent were 45 to 64, and 11.6 percent were 65 or older. The gender breakdown was 49.0 percent male and 51.0 percent female. Non-family households made up 56.4 percent of all households, and 36.2 percent consisted of individuals living alone.

The city has experienced steady population growth over recent decades, rising from 62,220 in the 2000 census to 67,862 in 2010 and 74,828 in 2020. In 2000, the median household income was 34,977 dollars, median family income was 57,568 dollars, and per capita income was 20,269 dollars. About 2.7 percent of families and 4.7 percent of the population were below the poverty line. As of 2010, approximately 58.0 percent of adults held a bachelor's degree or higher, and median household income was 41,410 dollars.

The University of Iowa, founded in 1847, is the defining institution of Iowa City. It enrolls approximately 31,000 students across more than 200 degree programs. The university includes a medical school and one of the largest university-owned teaching hospitals in the United States, providing care in 16 medical specialties. The Holden Comprehensive Cancer Center is one of fewer than 60 NCI-designated Cancer Centers nationwide. ACT Inc., developer of the ACT standardized college admissions test, operates its global headquarters in Iowa City with approximately 1,200 local employees.

Iowa City is served by the Iowa City Community School District, which operates four public high schools: Iowa City High School, Iowa City West High School, Liberty High School, and Elizabeth Tate Alternative High School. Regina Catholic Education Center provides private education. Kirkwood Community College also maintains a branch in the city.

In November 2008, UNESCO designated Iowa City as the world's third City of Literature, the first in the United States. The city is home to the renowned Iowa Writers' Workshop, the International Writing Program, and the Iowa Avenue Literary Walk. Cultural venues include the Englert Theatre, Riverside Theatre, and FilmScene.

Iowa City is governed by a seven-member elected city council under a council-manager form of government. The council appoints the city manager, who implements policy. The mayor is selected by the council from within its members to serve a two-year term. Transportation infrastructure includes Interstate 80, multiple U.S. and state highways, Iowa City Transit, Coralville Transit, and the University of Iowa Cambus system. The Eastern Iowa Airport, located 20 miles northwest, provides scheduled passenger service. The city has earned silver status as a bicycle-friendly community from the League of American Bicyclists.
